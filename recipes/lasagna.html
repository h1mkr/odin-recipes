Lasagna

Description

This lasagna recipe takes a little work, but it is so satisfying and filling that it's worth it! It is a type of pasta, possibly one of the oldest types, made of very wide, flat sheets. It is originated during the Middle Ages. It is one of the favorite dishes of the Italians.

How to Make Lasagna

Making lasagna can be time-consuming, but the results are well worth the wait. You'll find a detailed ingredient list and step-by-step instructions in the recipe below, but let's go over the basics:

Ingredients

  • Meat
  • Onion and Garlic
  • Tomato products
  • Sugar
  • Spices and seasonings
  • Lasagna noodles
  • Cheeses: Parmesan, mozzarella, and ricotta
  • Egg (optional)

Procedure

Here's a very brief overview of what you can expect when you make homemade lasagna:

  1. Make the meat sauce.
  2. Cook the pasta.
  3. Make the ricotta mixture.
  4. Layer the lasagna according to the recipe instructions.
  5. Cover with foil and bake.
  6. Let the lasagna rest before serving.

Layering the Lasagna

  1. Meat sauce
  2. Noodles
  3. Ricotta mixture
  4. Mozzarella slices
  5. Meat sauce
  6. Parmesan cheese
  7. Repeat the layers, then top with the remaining Parmesan.

How Long to Cook Lasagna

The assembled lasagna should take about 50 minutes to cook in an oven preheated to 375 degrees F. Cover it with foil for the first 25 minutes, then let it cook uncovered for the final 25 minutes. Also, it's important to let the lasagna rest at room temperature for about 15 minutes before you cut into it.

THAT'S IT! Your delicious lasagna is ready. Lasagna is best served with some Balsamic Grilled Zucchini
